## Diagnostic Conclusion

Three independent generator-only audits agreed that the retained implementation
must be replaced rather than rewired:

- it publishes individual files through pathname stages, backups, and in-memory
  rollback instead of one durable root transition;
- it has no persisted intent, canonical old/new inventories, stage registration,
  prepared/outcome markers, cleanup receipts, completed tombstone, or restart
  recovery;
- it stores arbitrary output paths and installs without a matching live lease;
- its owner-root hash locks do not converge on the canonical corpus and conflate
  immutable lock headers with mutable owner audit data;
- it has no rooted descriptor capability, exact-name/mount/fsid probes,
  fixed-mode no-follow mutation, supported-target gate, or read-only check guard;
- its public-style plan and lease must not return to the frozen crate surface;
- its excluded tests omit crash-prefix and deterministic race evidence and some
  assert obsolete rollback and opposite-domain behavior.
